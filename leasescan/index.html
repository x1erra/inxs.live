FAQ

Common questions

Can a landlord charge a security or damage deposit in Ontario?
No. Ontario landlords can only collect a last month's rent deposit. Any deposit called "security," "damage," "pet," or similar is illegal under Sections 105–106 of the RTA. If you already paid one, file a T1 application with the LTB to recover it plus interest.
Is a no-pets clause enforceable in Ontario?
No. No-pets clauses are legally unenforceable under Section 14 of the RTA. You have the right to keep a pet regardless of what the lease says. The only exception: condominiums where the condo corporation's declaration validly prohibits pets.
What is Bill 60 (2025) and why does it matter?
Ontario's Bill 60 (November 2025) is one of the most significant overhauls of the Residential Tenancies Act in years. Key changes include faster LTB processing for rent arrears, stricter bad-faith penalties for "landlord own use" (N12) evictions, standardized virtual hearings, and new tenant compensation entitlements. Most tenants are unaware these rules changed.
Is my lease text private? Does it get sent anywhere?
Your lease text never leaves your device. The entire analysis runs in JavaScript in your browser — nothing is transmitted to any server. The optional AI enhancement (Chrome only) also uses Google's on-device Gemini Nano model, which processes locally.
My lease has red flags. What should I do?
Don't panic — many landlords use old templates with illegal clauses by mistake. You have options: (1) Ask the landlord to remove or amend the clause before signing. (2) Sign anyway — illegal clauses are void even if you sign; the RTA always prevails over the lease. (3) Contact a free tenant legal clinic for advice. In Ontario: ACTO and LTB Duty Counsel provide free help.
Does this work for commercial leases?
No. LeaseScan is designed exclusively for residential tenancy law. Commercial leases are governed by entirely different legislation with far fewer tenant protections. Consult a commercial real estate lawyer for those.
